Angles

Types of Angles:

  • Acute angle: less than 90°
  • Right angle: exactly 90°
  • Obtuse angle: more than 90° and less than 180°
  • Straight angle: exactly 180°

Triangles and their Properties

Types of Triangles:

  • Acute triangle: all angles are acute
  • Right triangle: there is one right angle in the triangle
  • Obtuse triangle: there is one obtuse angle in the triangle
  • Equilateral triangle: all three sides of the triangle are the same length
  • Isosceles triangle: two of the sides of the triangle are the same length
  • Scalene triangle: all three sides of the triangle are different lengths

Properties of Triangles:

  • The sum of all three angles in a triangle is always 180°
  • The exterior angle of a triangle is always the sum of the other two interior angles
  • An equilateral triangle's angles are all 60°
  • The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right triangle, the sum of the square of the two side lengths adjacent to the right angle is equal to the square of the third side length.

Area Formulas

  • Square: side length * side length
  • Rectangle: width * height
  • Triangle: base * height / 2
  • Trapezoid: (base1 + base2) * height / 2
  • Circle: π * r^2

Perimeter Formulas

  • Square: side length * 4
  • Rectangle: (width + height) * 2
  • Triangle: sum of three side lengths
  • Trapezoid: sum of 4 side lengths
  • Circle: 2 * π * r
