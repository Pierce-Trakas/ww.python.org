Discrete Functions

Sequences

A sequence is a list of numbers connected by a pattern, usually either geometric or arithmetic. In an arithmetic sequence, there is a common difference between every number, and the formula is tn = a+d(n-1). Tn represents the nth term, a represents the initial value, and d represents the common difference. In a geometric sequence, there is a common ratio between every number, and the formula is tn = a(r)n-1. r represents the common ratio.

Series

To find out the sum of all numbers in a sequence (or all numbers up to a specific term), you have to translate that sequence into a series. The equation of an arithmetic series is Sn = n/2[2a+d(n-1)]. Sn represents the sum of a series. The equation of an arithmetic series is Sn = a(rn-1)/r-1.

Recursive Equations

Every sequence can also be expressed as a recursive equation, which includes previous terms as part of the formula. A famous recursive equation is the Fibonacci Sequence; which can be represented by the equation tn = tn-1 + tn-2, or "the nth term is equal to the sum of the two previous terms".

Expanding Binomials with Pascal's Triangle

When expanding a binomial in the format (a+b)n, the triangle made by famous mathematician Blaise Pascal can be used to make the process easier. To start, find the row of the triangle that corresponds to n's value (example in the image below). If n is 0, then the answer will always be 1. Once you have the row, use the numbers in that row as the coefficients for each term. The first term should be an, and the last should be bn. Every consecutive term, a's exponent should decrease by one, but b's should increase. Once you get every term (each structured as coefficient * an * bn), simplify them as much as possible. You have now expanded the binomial!
